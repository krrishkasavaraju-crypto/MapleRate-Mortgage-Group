FAQ

Common mortgage questions

Everything you need to know before starting your mortgage journey. Still have questions? Reach out and we will help.

Affordability depends on your income, existing debts, down payment, and credit profile. A general rule is that your total monthly housing costs (mortgage, property taxes, heating, and condo fees) should not exceed 32% of your gross monthly income. We run a full affordability analysis during your pre-approval to give you a clear, realistic number.

For the best rates, a credit score of 680 or higher is ideal. However, we work with lenders who accept scores as low as 600, and we have options for clients with limited credit history. Your credit score is just one factor — your income stability and down payment size also matter significantly.

Fixed rates offer stability and predictable payments, which is great if you value certainty. Variable rates typically start lower but can fluctuate with the market. The right choice depends on your risk tolerance, financial flexibility, and market outlook. We analyze both options against your specific situation to recommend the best fit.

With MapleRate, most clients receive their pre-approval within 72 hours of submitting their documents. For urgent situations, we can often expedite the process to 24 hours. Pre-approvals are typically valid for 90 to 120 days, giving you plenty of time to shop for your home with confidence.

Absolutely. Self-employed borrowers often face challenges with traditional banks, but we work with lenders who understand business income, use stated income programs, and accept alternative documentation. Whether you are a freelancer, contractor, or business owner, we have a solution that fits your income structure.

Refinancing penalties depend on your current mortgage terms. Fixed-rate mortgages typically have a penalty of the greater of three months interest or the interest rate differential (IRD). Variable-rate mortgages usually have a three-month interest penalty. We calculate these costs upfront and only recommend refinancing when the long-term savings clearly outweigh the penalty.
